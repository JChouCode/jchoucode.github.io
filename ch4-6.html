Chapter 4: Reactions in Aqueous Solutions

  • Electrolyte: Substance whose aqueous solutions contain ions
    • Most molecular compounds are nonelectrolytes, except acids
  • Solvation: When ions become surrounded by H2O molecules
    • Helps to stabilize the aqueous solution as the ions won’t recombine and are more evenly dispersed throughout
  • Strong electrolytes: Solutes that are nearly completely ions
    • Water soluble ionic compounds are usually strong electrolytes, except ones with the ammonium ion
  • Chemical Equilibrium: Balance between ionization and recombination where rates are constant
  • Precipitation: When an insoluble compound is formed
    • Usually when pairs of opposite ions attract each other
  • Solubility: Amount of substance that can be dissolved in a given quantity of solvent at a certain temperature
    • All common ionic compounds of the alkali metal group (1A) and ammonium are soluble in water
  • Metathesis reactions: Reactions where cations and anions exchange partners
  • Molecular equation: Shows the complete chemical formula of reactants and products
  • Complete ionic equation: Shows the electrolytes as ions
  • Net ionic equation: Chemical equation where spectator ions are omitted
    • Spectator ions: Ions that remain constant in reactants and products
    • Sum of charges from reactants and products must be equivalent
  • Acids and Bases
    • Acids: Substances that ionize in aqueous solution to form hydrogen ions H+
      • Called proton donors, H+ is simply a proton
      • Monoprotic, diprotic acids: Acids that produce a different amount of hydronium ions
    • Bases: Substance that react with H+ ions, producing hydroxide ions OH-
      • Not all bases need to contain OH, ex. NH3, however it is a weak base
      • Strong electrolytes result in strong bases and acids and vice versa
    • Acids and bases are harmful due to its ionizing power, it can affect the molecules in your skin by pulling electron and etc.
    • Strong Acids: HCl, HBr, HI, HClO3, HClO4, HNO3, H2SO4
    • Strong Bases: Group 1A Metal Hydroxides (ex. LiOH), Heavy group 2A metal hydroxides (ex. Ca(OH)2, Sr(OH)2)
    • Neutralization reaction: Acids and base combines to produce salt and water
      • Many bases react with H+ to form molecular compounds, resulting in gas formation
      • Sulfide Ions form hydrogen sulfide (H2S), giving off the smelly egg odor
      • Carbonates and bicarbonates react to form CO2
    • Antacid: Substances that remove extra stomach acid

Chapter 6: Electronic Structure of Atoms

  • Electromagnetic Radiation (Radiant Energy)
    • Moves at speed of light
    • Wavelike
    • Wavelength: Distance between two adjacent peaks
    • Frequency: Number of complete cycles each second
      • Hz: Unit s-1
    • C = (wavelength)(frequency)
  • Electromagnetic Spectrum
  • Blackbody Radiation: Emission of light from hot objects
  • Photoelectric effect: Emission of electrons from metal surfaces
    • Photon: Particle of energy
    • Energy of photon = hv
    • Transfers energy to electrons causing them to escape attractions
  • Emission Spectra: Emission of light from electronically excited atoms
  • Quantization of energy
    • Quantum: Smallest quantity of energy that can be emitted or absorbed as electromagnetic radiation
    • E = hv
      • H: Planck’s constant 6.626 x 10-23 joules-sec
  • Line Spectra
    • Spectrum: Produced when radiation is separated
    • Continuous spectrum: Rainbow of colors
    • Line spectrum: Spectrum containing radiation of specific wavelengths
    • 1/wavelength = RH(1/n1^2 - 1/n2^2)
      • RH: Rydberg constant (1.096776 x 10^7 m^-1)
      • N: Principle quantum number
  • Hydrogen Emission Spectrum
    • Lyman Series: n = 1
    • Balmer Series: n = 2
    • Paschen Series: n = 3
    • Brackett Series: n = 4
    • Pfund Series: n = 5
    • Humphrey Series: n = 6
  • Wave Behavior of Matter
    • De Broglie: wavelength = h/mv
    • Matter waves: Wave characteristics of material
